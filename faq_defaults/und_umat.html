What is the UMAT?

 

 

The UMAT stands for ‘Undergraduate Medical Admissions Test’.

 

The UMAT is an admissions test required for undergraduate-entry into

 

The UMAT is issued by the Australian Council for Educational Research(ACER) and consists of 3 sections:

 

  • Section 1: Logical Reasoning and Problem Solving (48 Questions in 70 Minutes)

  • Section 2: Understanding People (44 Questions in 55 Minutes)

  • Section 3: Non-Verbal Reasoning (42 Questions in 55 Minutes)

 

The questions are all in Multiple-Choice format and are weighted equally.

 

Please note that applicants for post-graduate entry into Medicine or Dentistry must take the Graduate Australian Medical School Admissions Test (GAMSAT) instead.

 

 

How frequently is the UMAT held?

 

 

The UMAT is held once a year. In 2015, it is held on 29 July 2015.

 

 

For how long are the results valid?

 

 

UMAT scores are valid for 1 year only.

 

For example, if you sit the exam during July 2015, the result will be valid for your 2015 application for a medical or health sciences program commencing in 2016

 

 

How many times can I take the UMAT?

 

 

There is no limit to the number of times you take the UMAT. If you were unsuccessful the first time, you can re-sit it the following year without being penalised provided you are still eligible to sit the test.

 

 

When should I sit the UMAT?

 

 

Since results are only valid for 1 year, you should sit for the UMAT the year before you intend to commence your relevant university course.

 

For example, if you sit the exam during July 2015, the result will be valid for your 2015 application for a medical or health sciences program commencing in 2016

 

 

What is the registration process for taking the UMAT?

 

 

Registrations for UMAT 2015 have been opened since December 2014.

 

You must complete your registration with the UMAT office at ACER no later than 5.00 pm Australian Eastern Standard Time (AEST) on Friday 5 June 2015.

 

 

I have missed the UMAT registration deadline. Will I be allowed to sit the UMAT?

 

 

If you intend to sit the UMAT in 2015, ACER allows for late registrations up to 5pm AEST on Friday 19 June 2015 on payment of alate fee of $65 in addition to

 

The online registration system closes at 5pm AEST on Friday 19 June 2015.You will NOT be allowed to register for the UMAT after this time.

 

 

Can I take a dictionary with me into the UMAT Exam?

 

 

No. The use of a dictionary is prohibited in any section of the test.

 

 

Can I take a calculator with me into the UMAT Exam?

 

 

No. A calculator is not allowed for any section of the test.

 

 

How much does the UMAT exam cost?

 

 

The UMAT costs $240 to sit and $140 if you have a valid concession card.

 

If you are sitting for the UMAT outside Australia or New Zealand, an additional overseas testing levy of $185 applies.
