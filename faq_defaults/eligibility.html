When can I sit the UMAT?


The UMAT is only available to students who are in their final year of secondary school, or students enrolled in university. Accordingly, students enrolled in 2015 in Australian Year 11 or lower are not eligible to sit UMAT 2015.

However this doesn’t mean that you can’t start preparing for the UMAT early!  


I am currently in Year 11 and enrolled in a Year 12 subject, can I sit the UMAT?


No. Year 11 students undertaking Year 12 subjects are NOT eligible to take the UMAT. Similarly, Year 12 students completing Year 12 over two or more years are only eligible to sit the UMAT in their final year.


Can I sit the UMAT if I have already completed a degree in university?


Yes, you will be eligible to sit for the UMAT regardless of whether you have commenced or completed an undergraduate degree.

However please note that not all undergraduate degree programs that require the UMAT as a prerequisite offers places for non-school leavers. All students are strongly encouraged to check the university’s relevant websites for further information.


Do International students need to take the UMAT?


International students are strongly advised to check the websites of the universities to which they plan to apply.

Only a limited number of universities actually require International students to sit the UMAT for entry into Medical and Health Sciences courses, and if so only in specific circumstances.

For example, entry into Medicine at Monash University requires candidates to take the International Students Admission Test (ISAT) as an alternative to the UMAT.
